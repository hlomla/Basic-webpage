Historical Places in Cape Town

Castle of Good Hope

  1. It’s the oldest existing colonial building in South Africa. Its position marks the original shoreline before years of land reclamation changed the Table Bay coastline.
  2. The materials used to build the Castle were local and included rock cut from the granite outcrop on Signal Hill, and blue slate and shells – transported from Robben Island
  3. Yellow paint was originally chosen for the walls because it reduced the effects of the hot African sun

City Hall

  1. Built in 1905, Cape Town's City Hall is the first place from where Nelson Mandela spoke after his release from prison in 1990.
  2. The building was designed as the result of a public competition, the winning architects being Messrs Harry Austin Reid and Frederick George Green
  3. On 24 July 2018, a statue of Nelson Mandela on the balcony overlooking the Grand Parade was unveiled. It was on the same spot where he made a speech when he was released from prison

Iziko Museum

  1. The South African Museum was founded in 1825. In 1897 the Museum moved to its present building in the historic Company's Garden.
  2. Iziko is a Xhosa word meaning "hearth".
  3. The museum houses important African zoology, palaeontology and archaeology collections.
